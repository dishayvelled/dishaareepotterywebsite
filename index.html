  History of Pottery


Pottery is one of the oldest human crafts, dating back over 20,000 years. Initially created for practical purposes like storage and cooking, it evolved into an art form with unique styles across cultures.

In India, pottery has a rich history, with evidence dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization. The Harappan culture’s intricate painted pottery laid the foundation for later traditions. Over time, Indian artisans mastered wheel-throwing, producing both functional and decorative pieces that reflect the country's cultural heritage.

In Bengal, Kumortuli in Kolkata is a renowned center for pottery, known for its clay sculptures and idols made for festivals like Durga Puja. The craft here is deeply spiritual, with artisans connecting with the earth and infusing energy into their creations.

At Dishaaree, we seek to bring this rich history and spirituality to life, offering a space where creativity and tradition come together. Through hands-on classes, we aim to help individuals connect with clay, explore their creativity, and honor timeless pottery practices.

  About Disha


Disha has always had an eye for creativity since childhood. With a degree in Computer Science Engineering, she pursued a technical path but soon realized her true passion lay in something more grounded.

Leaving behind her job in the tech industry, she decided to follow her heart and take a leap of faith towards her true calling. Embracing the connection to earth and the meditative process of pottery, Disha now brings her love for craftsmanship to life at her brain-child, Dishaaree.
